# ProCraft: Chain-of-Reasoning Audio Factorization for Transforming Production Style

## Abstract

We present ProCraft, a framework for instruction-guided music audio manipulation grounded in production reasoning. ProCraft consists of two contributions: (1) **ProCraft-Data**, a large-scale dataset (400K–2M entries) pairing original multitrack audio with production motivations, chain-of-thought reasoning traces, executable tool calls, and modified audio — generated by Qwen3-30B-A3B-Thinking over multitrack MIDI from Slakh2100, GigaMIDI, and the Lakh MIDI Dataset, rendered via FluidSynth with diverse soundfonts, processed with 85 effects from MultiAFX, and augmented with arrangement operations (track addition/removal); and (2) **ProCraft-Model**, a text-conditioned factorized codec that disentangles frozen DAC tokens into three factors — timbre [TMB], expression [EXP], and content — via periodic token injection, information bottleneck on content, gradient reversal, and a reconstruction-first curriculum where text prompt probability increases from 0% to 80% over training. During text-conditioned training, the instruction shifts supervision labels and reconstruction targets directly — no reference audio is needed at inference.

## 1. ProCraft-Data: Reasoning Music Production Dataset

### Source Material and Scale

We combine three multitrack MIDI pools: Slakh2100 (2.1K curated tracks), GigaMIDI (~922K multitrack files filtered for ≥3 tracks and expressivity), and Lakh MIDI (~170K multitrack files with MidiCaps annotations). Audio rendering uses a two-tier strategy: **Tier 1** — DawDreamer (headless Python DAW) with commercial VSTi instruments (EastWest ComposerCloud, Kontakt, Arturia) for high fidelity (8–9/10), multiprocessed with N=CPU-count workers; **Tier 2** — FluidSynth with curated soundfonts (Timbres of Heaven, SGM v2.01) for fast iteration and soundfont-diversity augmentation (4–5/10). Both tiers render per-stem with ITU-R BS.1770-4 loudness normalization. Augmented with tempo variation (±10%), transposition (±1–3 semitones), and track subsetting, yielding ~50K–100K unique multitrack mixtures.

### Generation Pipeline

Each entry contains: (original\_audio, production\_motivation, thinking\_trace, tool\_calls, modified\_audio). **Qwen3-30B-A3B-Thinking** (30.5B params, 3.3B active MoE, 256K context) receives audio metadata (instrument labels, tempo, key) and a system prompt defining all available tools in Hermes-style XML. The model natively produces `<think>` reasoning before `<tool_call>` outputs.

**Four tool categories** cover the full breadth of music production:

| Category | Tools | What Changes |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| **A. Instrument** | `change_instrument`, `layer_instrument` | Timbre (re-render via DawDreamer+VSTi or FluidSynth) |
| **B. Effects** | 85 effects from MultiAFX (EQ, dynamics, reverb, distortion, modulation, spatial, spectral) | Timbre |
| **C. Performance** | `change_velocity`, `humanize_timing`, `change_articulation` | Expression |
| **D. Arrangement** | `add_track`, `remove_track`, `double_track`, `mute_and_replace` | Content + Timbre |

Category D is critical for generality: it enables content-modifying production decisions (e.g., "the arrangement is thin — add a string pad"), making ProCraft-Data a general production reasoning dataset rather than a timbre-only one. Track addition is implemented by withholding a track from the original mix and adding it back via tool call; removal omits a stem from the modified output.

Quality filtering discards entries where FAD exceeds 2× median (too destructive) or falls below 0.05 (inaudible). Final scale: **400K–2M entries** after filtering.

### Comparison to Prior Datasets

| Dataset | Scale | Effects | Reasoning | Inst. Change | Arrangement | Perf. Mod |
|---------|-------|---------|-----------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| AUDIT (NeurIPS 2023) | ~45K | 8 ops | None | No | No | No |

## 2. ProCraft-Model: Text-Conditioned Factorized Codec

### Architecture

ProCraft-Model operates on frozen DAC RVQ tokens. A unified encoder, inspired by ALMTokenizer's periodic token injection, processes the sequence:

```
[text_emb₁..text_embₘ, z̃₁..z̃₈, [TMB]₁, [EXP]₁, z̃₉..z̃₁₆, [TMB]₂, [EXP]₂, ...]
```

where `z̃` denotes ~20% randomly masked DAC tokens. Text embeddings (from frozen T5-small + MLP adapter) are prepended. After 12 self-attention layers, a retriever separates outputs by position into three factor streams:

- **[TMB]** (timbre): mixture-level instrument composition (e.g., {violin, bass, drums})
- **[EXP]** (expression): dynamics, effects character, articulation — everything not timbre or content
- **Content**: pitch/rhythm, passed through an 8-dim information bottleneck predicting pianoroll

The decoder interleaves the three factors per window and predicts RVQ tokens → frozen DAC decoder → audio.

### Disentanglement (always active, all modes)

| Factor | Supervision | Mechanism |
|--------|------------|-----------|
| [TMB] | Multi-label instrument classification + GRL against pianoroll | BCE against mixture-level inst. labels; GRL strips content from timbre |
| Content | Pianoroll prediction | 8-dim information bottleneck + GRL against instrument classifier |
| [EXP] | Residual (everything else) | Dual GRL against both instrument classifier and pianoroll predictor |

All supervision losses are always active. FACodec-inspired perturbation (pitch-shift for timbre invariance, MIDI shuffle for content invariance) supplements the GRL.

### Text-Conditioned Training

The text instruction shifts **what labels supervise the factor tokens** and **what audio is the reconstruction target** — no reference audio is encoded separately.

| Mode | [TMB] Label | Content Label | Recon Target |
|------|------------|---------------|-------------|
| Reconstruction (no text) | Source instruments | Source pianoroll | Original audio |
| Instrument change | **Modified** instruments | Source pianoroll | Modified audio |
| Effects change | **Modified** (timbre altered) | Source pianoroll | Modified audio |
| Performance change | Source instruments | Source pianoroll | Modified audio |
| Add track | **Modified** instruments | **Modified** pianoroll | Modified audio |
| Remove track | **Modified** instruments | **Modified** pianoroll | Modified audio |

Timbre labels are always mixture-level: swapping piano→violin in a {piano, bass, drums} mix produces the target label {violin, bass, drums}.

### Curriculum

Text prompt probability increases gradually to ensure disentanglement is learned before text conditioning:

| Phase | Steps | Text Prob | Focus |
|-------|-------|-----------|-------|
| Disentanglement | 0–90K | 0% | Reconstruction + factor separation |
| Ramp | 90K–150K | 0→50% | Introduce text conditioning |
| Joint | 150K–240K | 50% | Full training |
| Consolidation | 240K–300K | 80% | Emphasize text-conditioned mode |

### Inference

```python
def craft_inference(audio, instruction, instrument_metadata):
    z = dac_encode(audio)
    text_emb = mlp(t5_encode(f"{instrument_metadata} | {instruction}"))
    encoded = unified_encoder(build_sequence(text_emb, mask(z, 0.0), w=8))
    t_tmb, t_exp, c = retrieve_by_position(encoded)
    rvq_tokens = decoder(interleave(t_tmb, t_exp, c))
    return dac_decode(rvq_tokens)
```

At inference, the encoder has learned to shift [TMB] and [EXP] tokens based on the text instruction — producing factor representations consistent with the described modification, without reference audio.

## 3. Evaluation

### Baselines

1. **No-Disentanglement ablation**: Same capacity, same text encoder, but monolithic (no [TMB]/[EXP], no GRL, no bottleneck). Isolates the factorization contribution.
2. **Instruct-MusicGen** (ISMIR 2025): Instruction-tuned MusicGen for stem editing.
3. **AUDIT** (NeurIPS 2023): Instruction-guided audio editing via latent diffusion.
4. **InstructME** (IJCAI 2024): Text-conditioned music editing, monolithic latent.

### Metrics

| Metric | Measures | Target |
|--------|----------|--------|
| FAD | Perceptual quality (VGGish) | ≤ baseline |
| KAD | Complementary perceptual quality (MMD kernel) | ≤ baseline |
| Content Preservation (CPR) | Pianoroll F1 vs. target MIDI | ≥ baseline + 10 F1 |
| Instrument Classification (ICA) | Timbre coherence | ≥ 0.75 |
| MOS | Human perceptual rating (5-point) | Supplementary |

### Ablation Studies

| # | Ablation | Tests |
|---|----------|-------|
| 1 | No GRL on content | GRL vs. bottleneck alone |
| 2 | No dual-GRL on [EXP] | Expression disentanglement |
| 3 | No GRL on [TMB] against pianoroll | Timbre-content separation |
| 4 | No information bottleneck (probe-only) | Structural vs. probe bottleneck |
| 5 | No FACodec perturbation | Perturbation contribution |
